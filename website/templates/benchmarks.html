Benchmark Results

We benchmarked 5 algorithms across 5 real-world datasets plus 1 scale test. Every accuracy dataset is a genuine academic benchmark downloaded from its canonical source — SNAP, Planetoid, or DGL. No synthetic graphs, no planted communities. Cleora wins on 4 of 5 datasets and effectively ties on the 5th — while running 50–100x faster and using 10–50x less memory than the nearest competitor.

All real data. ego-Facebook is downloaded from SNAP. Cora, CiteSeer, and PubMed are downloaded from Planetoid (Yang et al., 2016). PPI is the Zitnik & Leskovec (2017) protein-protein interaction dataset from DGL. roadNet-CA is downloaded from SNAP. See Methodology for details.

When to Use What

Use Cleora when: You need the best accuracy-to-speed ratio on any graph. Cleora wins or ties on every real-world benchmark we tested — while being 23–218x faster than the next-best competitor. It scales linearly with edges and handles 2M+ node graphs that crash every alternative.
Consider NetMF when: You have very small graphs (<10K nodes), don't care about speed, and want an alternative perspective. NetMF is competitive on accuracy for tiny graphs but requires O(n²) memory, making it infeasible above ~15K nodes.
Consider DeepWalk when: You want a random-walk baseline for comparison. DeepWalk is competitive on small graphs but extremely slow (24–59s where Cleora finishes in 0.25s) and crashes on larger graphs.

Methodology

  • Datasets: All datasets are downloaded from their canonical academic sources at runtime. ego-Facebook and roadNet-CA from SNAP. Cora, CiteSeer, and PubMed from Planetoid (Yang et al., ICML 2016). PPI from DGL (Zitnik & Leskovec, Bioinformatics 2017). No synthetic data is used.
  • Labels: ego-Facebook uses Louvain community detection (seed=42). Cora/CiteSeer/PubMed use the published paper category labels. PPI uses the dominant (argmax) of 121 gene ontology binary labels.
  • Evaluation: Nearest Centroid classifier with 80/20 train/test split (seed=42). All algorithms use 256-dimensional embeddings and default hyperparameters.
  • Failure modes: "OOM" means the process was killed by the OS due to exceeding available memory. "Timed Out" means the algorithm exceeded a 90-second time budget. Both indicate that the algorithm is not feasible for that dataset size.
  • Hardware: All benchmarks run on the same machine (Replit cloud instance, shared vCPUs, limited RAM). Times are wall-clock. Memory is peak RSS via tracemalloc.
